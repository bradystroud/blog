# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Potential clients, employers, collaborators, and fellow developers who want to understand what Brady builds, how he thinks, and the breadth of his practical engineering work.

## Product Purpose

Brady Stroud's personal website combines a technical blog with a working portfolio. It should make Brady's experience and interests tangible through useful writing, shipped projects, and a candid view of the person behind the code. Success means a visitor can quickly understand his strengths, find work relevant to them, and leave with confidence that he builds thoughtful software that solves real problems.

## Brand Personality

Clear, practical, and quietly opinionated. The voice is approachable and direct, with technical credibility that comes from showing real work instead of making inflated claims.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic developer portfolio templates, repetitive feature-card grids, empty buzzwords, neon hacker styling, and corporate marketing language. The site should not feel like a startup landing-page template or a résumé copied onto the web.

## Design Principles

- Show shipped work and concrete outcomes instead of relying on broad claims.
- Make technical depth easy to scan without flattening it into jargon.
- Let Brady's personality appear through specific details and plainspoken copy.
- Use confident hierarchy and restraint so the work remains the focus.
- Keep every page useful to both technical and non-technical visitors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic structure, sufficient contrast in light and dark themes, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion preferences.
